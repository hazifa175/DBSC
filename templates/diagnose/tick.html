Ticks

Ticks survive on the blood of host animals, and as such, are common on animals with fur in which they can hide. There are 2 classifications of a tick: hard- and soft-bodied species. Depending on their geographic location, ticks may be carriers of diseases, most commonly Lyme Disease, which often occurs in densely wooded areas with large deer populations. In the Fresno area, anaplasmosis and ehrlichia—both tick-borne diseases—are also common.

Care Advices

1. Remove tick from your dog using tweezers.
2. Check your dog's hair regularly.
3. Use oral medications
4. Put tick collar on dog's neck to get rid of the tick
5. Spray the tick sprays, you can find in pet stores
6.Avoid dog from areas in the height of tick season.
