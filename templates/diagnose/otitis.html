Otitis

Otitis externa is inflammation of the external ear canal and is a common problem in dogs and cats. Signs can include head shaking, pain, malodor, erythema, erosion, ulceration, swelling, and/or ceruminous gland inflammation.

Care Advices

1. Manage underlying causes of otitis external
2. Select antimicrobials based on history and cytology
3. Identify the primary cause of the otitis and manage it
4. Remove the exudate via irrigation of the ear canal
5. Identify and treat concurrent otitis media
6. select an appropriate antibiotic from the results of culture and mean inhibitory concentration on the organism and use it at an effective dosage for an appropriate duration
